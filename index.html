Protect Yourself from Keyloggers

Learn what they are, how they work, and how to stay safe.

What is a Keylogger?

A keylogger is a malicious program or device that records every keystroke you type on a computer or mobile device. This includes passwords, credit card numbers, and personal messages.

Where are they found?

They can be software hidden in email attachments, fake apps, or pirated games. They can also be physical devices plugged into public computers (like at libraries or internet cafes).

How to stay safe?

  • Use a virtual keyboard for sensitive data.
  • Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Check USB ports on public computers.
  • Keep your antivirus updated.
